# Goal
Define the product's strategic point of view so later design and engineering choices stay coherent.

# When to Use
- The idea is promising but directionally fuzzy.
- Feature discussions need a guiding principle.
- The team wants a differentiated product posture.

# Instructions
1. Identify target user, desired transformation, and trust promise.
2. Write a one-line product thesis.
3. Derive 3-5 practical product principles.
4. Define what the product should intentionally avoid in v1.
5. Tie philosophy to likely UX and architecture implications.

# Constraints
- Keep principles operational, not inspirational.
- Avoid generic startup slogans.
- Do not hide tradeoffs.

# Output Format
- Product thesis
- Product principles
- Differentiation
- Non-goals
- Strategic implications

# Examples
- "What should this AI ops product stand for?"
- "Define the philosophy for a premium developer tool."
