# Design Rule

All visual output must follow these principles:

## 1. Communication First

Design must prioritize clarity of message over visual styling.

## 2. Structure Before Style

Always define layout, hierarchy, and spacing before applying colors or visuals.

## 3. Typography Discipline

- use at most one or two font families
- maintain clear hierarchy
- ensure readability at all sizes

## 4. Contrast Control

Use contrast intentionally to guide attention.

## 5. Grid And Alignment

All layouts must follow a consistent grid and alignment system.

## 6. Medium Awareness

Design must adapt to:
- screen using RGB
- print using CMYK
- responsive digital contexts

## 7. Simplicity

Remove unnecessary elements.
Clarity is more important than complexity.

## 8. Consistency

Spacing, typography, and colors must stay consistent across the system.

## 9. Purpose-Driven Design

Every element must serve:
- communication
- usability
- business goals or user goals

## 10. No Decoration-Only Design

Visuals must support meaning, not exist for aesthetics alone.
